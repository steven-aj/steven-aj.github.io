## Getting Ready

> After [collecting 12 different types of material in a content audit](/blog/12-types-of-material-to-collect-in-a-content-audit), the next logical step is building a sitemap.

Once you've fragmented and organized your project's content, it's time to collaborate with the client on which pieces they want featured. By this point, you should have enough information to determine whether their project is better suited as a one-off landing page or a multi-page site. Since everything is already contextually organized, you can begin creating a sitemap to give yourself (*and the client*) a rough idea of the scope of work. Since landing pages are relatively straightforward, you should find it fairly easy to navigate structuring one with your client. If you need help, analyzing the way their competitors organize their own materials can give you some insight on how to structure these.

### Multi-Site Contexts



You can use any mind-mapping or graphing app to generate a sitemap, though there are some affordable (*and some free*) tools built specifically for the job.
